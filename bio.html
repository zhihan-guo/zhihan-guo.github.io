I am a PhD student in the Computational Media Lab at the Australian National University. I am also affiliated with CSIRO Data61. My advisors are Prof. Lexing Xie, Dr. Marian-Andrei Rizoiu, and Dr. Cheng Soon Ong.

I am broadly interested in measuring and modeling collective social behavior on web context. Some previous work includes how YouTube recommender systems drive user attention, why some YouTube vidoes are more engaging, and how Twitter sampling impacts common measurements. My work lies at the fields of computational social science, social computing, and data mining.

Prior to my PhD, I obtained my M.S. (Information Technology) and B.E. (Electronics Engineering) from the University of Melbourne and Tianjin University, respectively. I have also worked in MicroStrategy and Baidu.
